## What Didn't Go Well

### 1. Non-Null Assertion Pattern Recurred Across Stories
`result.data!` was flagged in code reviews for Stories 2.1, 2.3, and 2.4.
- **Root Cause:** Pattern not preventively documented after first occurrence
- **Impact:** Unnecessary review iterations on avoidable issues

### 2. Gesture-Based Accessibility Actions Repeatedly Missed
`accessibilityActions` was missing for swipe gestures in Story 2.3 (HIGH finding) and for toggle status in Story 2.7 (HIGH finding).
- **Root Cause:** No checklist prompting gesture accessibility when implementing gesture-based features
- **Impact:** Would have shipped without screen reader support without review

### 3. Story 2.3 Required 3 Review Rounds
The most complex story (navigation stack + swipe gestures + form pre-fill) required 3 review rounds with 12+ fixes.
- **Root Cause:** High story complexity; multiple new patterns introduced simultaneously
- **Impact:** Most costly story from a review iteration perspective

### 4. Test Framework Setup Complexity (Story 2.2)
Jest 30 incompatible with jest-expo@55 — required downgrade to Jest 29.7.0. react-test-renderer version mismatch required pinning to 19.1.0.
- **Root Cause:** External library compatibility not verified pre-implementation
- **Pattern Note:** Paper Snackbar uses internal timing incompatible with `jest.advanceTimersByTime()` — must use `jest.runAllTimers()` instead

### 5. Story 2.8 Critical Finding: UndoSnackbar After Detail Delete
After deleting from SubscriptionDetailScreen, the UndoSnackbar in SubscriptionsScreen wasn't showing.
- **Root Cause:** pendingDelete state observer not wired in SubscriptionsScreen for detail-initiated deletes
- **Impact:** Would have shipped silently without undo functionality

## Key Insights & Learnings

1. **Pattern maturity reduces errors** — Story 2.6's "no issues" result shows that once patterns are fully established and referenced, implementation quality improves dramatically.

2. **Preventive > Detective for recurring issues** — Non-null assertions and gesture accessibility were caught reactively. A pre-submission checklist would catch these before review.

3. **Test infrastructure cost is front-loaded** — Story 2.2's setup complexity was significant, but the framework enabled rapid, consistent test writing for stories 2.3–2.8.

4. **Complex stories need extra review budget** — Story 2.3 (3 rounds) vs Story 2.6 (0 issues) shows that story complexity is the strongest predictor of review overhead.

5. **Optimistic UI + Zustand is proven** — The delete-with-undo and status-toggle patterns are stable, well-tested, and ready for reuse in future stories.
